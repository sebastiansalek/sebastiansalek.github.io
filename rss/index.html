Some clever people have capitalised on that gripe, launching services like TwitLonger and Storify. However, sometimes we just want our followers to know that what we're posting is part of a longer message spanning multiple tweets.

This is most commonly done in two ways:

1) Numbering

This method isn't ideal because it takes up characters and orders the tweets in reverse order.

2) Typing quickly and hoping for the best

This method will split your tweets up on some people's timelines (if someone else they follow tweets between them) and so they'll be meaningless out of context. Even if they don't, the message string will be displayed chronologically, so people will end up reading your last message first.

3) The third option

There's actually a third option that's better than both of these methods, and it involves those pesky blue lines you all hated at first but have now forgotten about.

Post your first tweet, then as you go to type the second, reply to the previous one. Even if you delete your handle (which is recommended), blue lines will appear linking them together.

This saves you characters as you don't have to include numbering or ellipses, it keeps the tweets linked on your followers' timelines no matter how far apart you tweet, and it displays them in the right order to be read.

Happy tweeting!
